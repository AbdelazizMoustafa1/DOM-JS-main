    Introduction

    Planet Earth is full of chemicals and elements which are essentially useful and can be used and structured to different needs. These raw elements are present in different ways and combinations, and some can be readily usable or needs some processing before being used. Extracting elements from their ores or their natural resources can be done until the resource is empty or no longer able to provide the substance like in the case of crude oil wells. Recycling then allows elements to be used multiple times after different applications if the element is not used up.

    Historical Overview

    The worldwide vision of recycling and saving natural resources is showing its effect, not only within the manufacturing and chemical industries but also among sports and fun activities, which at the same time minimize costs and efforts. A recent example of the latter is the Tokyo 2020 Olympics games which were finally held during the summer of 2021, in which the medals were entirely made of recycled electronics. The gold, which is gold-plated silver, pure silver and bronze, which is 95% copper and 5% zinc, medals were made from recycled electronic devices, like cell phones, laptops, cameras and others.

    Future

    Different methods and techniques are studied for their reliability and efficiency in recovery and separation of REEs from various materials and extracts. These methods include pre-selection, separation, physical and chemical treatment and individual REEs separation techniques such as adsorption, ionic liquids and membranes applications. Membranes based on modified polymer matrices such as polymers, hybrid, ion-imprinted polymers are drawing a huge interest among researchers for their high selectivity and affinity and technical compatibility.
